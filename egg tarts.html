egg tarts: a recipe!

hey stranger! so you want to make some egg tarts, huh?

good choice! egg tarts are pretty great, and they have an interesting history as well! the origins of egg tarts start in england and portugal, way back in the 18th century, with the creation of the english custard tart and pastel de nata, respectively. after being introduced to these tarts, the chinese port city of guangzhou also began to make their own version of egg custard tarts, called dan ta! now, the custard tart is a dessert that's both a european delicacy, as well as a staple food in many asian cultures :)


first off: ingredients!

1. 280 g plain flour

2. 2 tablespoons icing sugar

3. 6 eggs

5. 150 g salted butter

6. 150 g castor/fine sugar

7. 150 g hot water

9. 250 ml milk

10. 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

now, let's start baking!

1. to start off, you'll want to wash your hands first. please. no dirty hands allowed!

2. we'll start with the pastry. mix your butter, icing sugar, and two eggs together.

3. next, add in your flour, and mix it all together. press and knead your mixture to form a dough.

4. wrap your dough in clingwrap, and cool in your refrigerator.

5. after 15 minutes, take your dough out, and cut it into twelve equal pieces.

6. roll each piece into a ball! this is the best part :) round :)

6. then, flatten your dough ball and place it into a tart case, pressing lightly.

7. finally, chill your tart cases for 30 minutes.

your pastry is done, and now, you can move onto the filling!


1. start by adding the castor sugar into the hot water, and mix until the sugar has dissolved. set this solution aside, and let it cool.

2. add your milk and vanilla extract to the rest of the eggs, and beat the mixture.

3. pour your cooled sugar-water solution into the eggs, and mix them well together.

now, you just have to put two parts together!


1. take your pastries out of the refrigerator, and pour some filling into each shell. satisfying, isn't it?

2. then, bake your tarts for 25 minutes at 180 degrees.

now, you're done! enjoy your egg tarts :) remember to like and subscribe and comment down below
